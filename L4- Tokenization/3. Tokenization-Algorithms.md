# 1. Why Do We Need Tokenization Algorithms?

## Definition

A **tokenization algorithm** is a method used to automatically build a tokenizer's vocabulary and define how text should be split into tokens.

A tokenizer cannot simply split text by spaces because languages are far more complex.

---

## Problem with Splitting by Spaces

Suppose we tokenize by spaces.

```text
I love programming
```

↓

```text
["I","love","programming"]
```

Looks fine.

Now consider

```text
playing

played

player

playground

replaying
```

Every variation becomes a different token.

This creates a huge vocabulary.

---

## Another Problem

Suppose tomorrow someone invents

```text
HyperQuantumAI
```

A space-based tokenizer has never seen this word.

It cannot tokenize it correctly.

This is called the **Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV)** problem.

---

## Solution

Instead of storing every word,

store common pieces of words.

Example

```text
play

ing

er

ground

re
```

Now

```text
replaying
```

becomes

```text
re

play

ing
```

The tokenizer can understand new words by combining known pieces.

This idea is the foundation of modern tokenization algorithms.

---

# 2. How is a Tokenizer Trained?

Many people think tokenizers are handwritten.

They are not.

A tokenizer is learned from data.

---

## Step 1

Collect a very large text corpus.

Example

```text
Wikipedia

Books

GitHub

Research Papers

News
```

---

## Step 2

Run a tokenization algorithm.

The algorithm discovers

- common words
- common subwords
- common character combinations

---

## Step 3

Build

- vocabulary
- merge rules (for BPE)
- token IDs

The tokenizer is now complete.

Only after this does LLM training begin.

---

# 3. Byte Pair Encoding (BPE)

## Definition

Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) is a tokenization algorithm that builds a vocabulary by repeatedly merging the most frequent adjacent symbols.

Originally developed as a data compression algorithm, it was later adapted for NLP.

GPT-2 uses BPE.

---

# Why was BPE introduced?

Character tokenization

↓

Too many tokens

Word tokenization

↓

Too many unique words

BPE

↓

Uses common subwords.

---

# How BPE Works

Suppose our training corpus contains

```text
low

lower

lowest
```

---

## Step 1

Split into characters.

```text
l o w

l o w e r

l o w e s t
```

---

## Step 2

Count adjacent pairs.

Example

```text
l o

o w

w e

e r

e s

s t
```

Suppose

```text
l o
```

appears most frequently.

Merge it.

---

Now

```text
lo w

lo w e r

lo w e s t
```

Again count pairs.

Suppose

```text
lo w
```

is most frequent.

Merge.

↓

```text
low

low e r

low e s t
```

Continue until the vocabulary reaches the desired size.

---

## What are Merge Rules?

Every merge is stored.

Example

```text
Merge 1

l + o

↓

lo
```

Merge 2

```text
lo + w

↓

low
```

These rules are saved.

During inference,

the tokenizer applies the same merge rules to new text.

---

# Real-Time Example

Suppose

```text
playing
```

Vocabulary

```text
play

ing
```

The tokenizer produces

```text
play

ing
```

instead of

```text
p

l

a

y

i

n

g
```

---

# Advantages

✔ Small vocabulary

✔ Handles new words

✔ Efficient

---

# Limitations

Sometimes produces unnatural splits.

Example

```text
internation

alization
```

instead of

```text
international

ization
```

# 4. WordPiece

## Definition

WordPiece is a tokenization algorithm developed for BERT.

Instead of merging the most frequent pairs,

it merges pairs that maximize the probability of the training corpus.

In simple words,

it chooses merges that improve language modeling the most.

---

## Example

```text
playing
```

↓

```text
play

##ing
```

The prefix

```text
##
```

means

"This token continues the previous word."

---

## Used By

- BERT
- DistilBERT

---

# Difference from BPE

BPE

↓

Most frequent pair wins.

WordPiece

↓

Most useful pair wins.

---

# 5. SentencePiece

## Definition

SentencePiece is a tokenizer that treats the input as a sequence of Unicode characters rather than relying on whitespace.

It works well for languages where spaces are optional or absent.

---

## Why Was It Introduced?

Some languages

```text
Chinese

Japanese

Thai
```

do not separate words with spaces.

Word-based tokenization fails.

SentencePiece works directly on raw text.

---

## Example

Instead of

```text
Split by spaces
```

SentencePiece learns segmentation automatically.

---

## Used By

- Llama
- T5
- ALBERT
- mT5

---

# 6. Byte-Level BPE

## Definition

Byte-Level BPE performs tokenization at the byte level before applying BPE merges.

Instead of starting with characters,

it starts with bytes.

---

## Why?

Characters differ across languages.

Bytes are universal.

Everything

- English
- Tamil
- Chinese
- Emoji
- Code

can be represented as bytes.

---

## Example

Emoji

```text
😀
```

Even if the tokenizer has never seen it,

its bytes are valid.

Therefore,

no unknown characters exist.

---

## Used By

GPT-2

GPT-3

GPT-4 family (variants use byte-level or related approaches)

---

# 7. Comparison

| Feature | BPE | WordPiece | SentencePiece |
|---------|-----|-----------|---------------|
| Vocabulary learned automatically | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Uses spaces | Yes | Yes | No |
| Handles unseen words | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Works well for multilingual text | Moderate | Moderate | Excellent |
| Used by | GPT-2 | BERT | Llama, T5 |

| Model | Algorithm |
|--------|-----------|
| GPT-2 | Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) |
| GPT-3 | Byte-Level BPE |

# Common Interview Questions

### Why can't we tokenize by spaces?

Because languages contain millions of unique words, and new words appear constantly. Space-based tokenization leads to an enormous vocabulary and the Out-of-Vocabulary problem.

---

### What problem does BPE solve?

It reduces vocabulary size while still allowing unseen words to be represented using common subwords.

---

### What are merge rules?

Merge rules are the ordered list of symbol combinations learned during BPE training. They are reused every time text is tokenized.

---

### Difference between BPE and WordPiece?

BPE merges the most frequent symbol pair.

WordPiece merges the pair that best improves the probability of the training data.

---

### Why is SentencePiece popular?

Because it does not depend on whitespace and works well for multilingual languages.

---

### Why does GPT use Byte-Level BPE?

Using bytes guarantees that any Unicode text can be represented without unknown characters.

---

# Common Misconceptions

❌ BPE is a tokenizer.

✅ BPE is an algorithm used to build a tokenizer.

---

❌ Tokenization algorithms are used every time the model trains.

✅ The tokenizer is trained once. The learned vocabulary and rules are then reused.

---

❌ All LLMs use BPE.

✅ Different models choose different algorithms based on their goals.

---

# Summary

- Tokenization algorithms learn how text should be split.
- They build the vocabulary before LLM training begins.
- BPE repeatedly merges the most frequent adjacent symbols.
- WordPiece chooses merges based on probability rather than frequency.
- SentencePiece learns directly from raw text without relying on spaces.
- Byte-Level BPE starts from bytes, making it robust for any Unicode text.
- Modern LLMs rely on subword tokenization because it balances vocabulary size, efficiency, and flexibility.
